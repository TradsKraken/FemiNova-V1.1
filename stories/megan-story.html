Megan's Triumph

From abuse to independence: Megan's story of courage and new beginnings

Back to Stories

Story Summary

Megan endured ten years of physical and emotional abuse from her partner, including punches, monitoring of her every move, and isolation from family and friends. At age 32, she made the courageous decision to leave for the safety of herself and her four-year-old daughter. Through CAWC's support services including counseling and relocation assistance, Megan rebuilt her life, achieving financial independence and a loving relationship.

Megan's Full Story

Megan's story is one of courage, resilience, and transformation. After enduring a decade of abuse, she found the strength to break free and build a new life filled with hope and possibility.

The Breaking Point

It has been ten years since Megan left her abuser. The decision was easier than the actual act of leaving. But she had a four-year-old daughter and a newborn. She feared for her life and for theirs. She loved them both with her whole heart and knew that she couldn't raise them in an abusive home.

"I had to get away from my abuser, But how?"

For years, Megan was physically and emotionally abused. She sustained bruises from his punches. He monitored her every move. He isolated her from her family and friends. He ensured that she was financially dependent on him. He exerted control and power over her. Control and power are an abuser's mantra.

Finding Help

Megan felt hopeless and afraid. Her self-esteem plummeted the longer she stayed with him. She lost any dignity that she had. And one night when she was at the breaking point, she called the hotline and started individual counseling at CAWC. She attended a survivor's support group and met others like her, others who endured horrific abuse.

"Because of my CAWC counselor and the domestic violence survivors in my group, I found the courage to leave my abuser."

The Journey to Freedom

Coming to CAWC for help was the best decision that Megan ever made. It wasn't easy. She left with two small children and not much more. But when punches got too close to her children, she knew that she had to move forward and escape a terrible life.

Ten years later, he still harasses and threatens her. But because of what she learned at CAWC, she now can deflect his words and his actions. She is engaged to a wonderful, caring man who loves her sons. She shares her story with others, and she knows that her words have convinced them to leave their abusers.

A Life Reclaimed

Megan has a wonderful job at a well-respected company. Her sons are thriving at school and in life. She's lucky. She tells herself that she survived for a reason - to live an amazing life, a life filled with happiness and love and not with fear - a life that she deserves.

"Thank you for listening to my story. And I hope that you can support CAWC - I probably wouldn't be here today without them."

Key Lessons & Impact

  • Children are powerful motivators for leaving abusive relationships
  • Professional counseling and support groups provide essential tools for healing
  • Financial dependence is a key barrier that keeps survivors trapped
  • Survivors can rebuild beautiful lives filled with love, success, and purpose
  • Sharing stories saves lives - survivors' voices can inspire others to seek help
